Cyborg box

Photo by J.Pihlgren
Description

The Cyborg Box is an exhibition installation in the form of a maze. The maze measures but 5 x 2 meters and should be easy to navigate, only, visitors can't use their eyes. Instead they are provided with a welders helmet with the visor completely blocking their vision. To their aid, the helmets are fitted with ultra sonic range finders that measure the distance to any objects facing the sensor. This distance is then used to trigger vibration motors. The closer to an object the sensor is, the more the vibration motor vibrates.

The box was exhibited at IDXPO and the AHA Festival. It will also be featured at The International Science Festival Gothenburg in April 2020.

The project
What
An installation for an exhibiton with the theme, showing the invisible.
Why
Curb the decline in interest in technology in one of the target groups at Universeum. Create a tangible means of experiencing things humans usually can't see.
Learning outcome
Introduce the notion of integrating artificial senses into the public understanding of perception, as well as comparing different means of perceiving the world.
The target group
  • Highschool students
  • Aged 15-17
