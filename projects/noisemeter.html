Noisemeter

Graphical Design, left, by Sjoerd Hendricks and Erik Bennerhed . Digital implementation in Wear OS, right, by Tommy Olsson, Robin Edquist, Daniel Illipe and Joachim Pihlgren
Description

The noisemeter is the result of a project in mobile computing. It's a partially implemented conceptual design which aims to decrease fatique due to noise exposure. Here we were split up in two teams.The design team produced digital designs which were handed over to the programming team. When an implementation was finished, it was evaluated and adjustments were made.

The project
What
A smart watch app that records decibels and lets you know when you've been exposed to certain thresholds of noise.
Why
Noise is a "silent" danger. When acting in a noisy environment, it is easy to acclimate and not realize that the environment is noisy. This can lead to fatigue and even hearing impairment.
Aim
Provide people with a way to keep track of noise exposure and allow them to make conscious choices of when to leave noisy environments.
The target group
  • People acting in noisy environments
  • Ages 15 - 65
